## The Rise

When was the first time I saw ChatGPT? Yes, it was early 2023.

It was a quiet morning, and the company Slack channels were as dull as ever. As I was casually browsing, I noticed a new thread in the technology noise channel.
Someone was asking if anyone had used ChatGPT, and what their thoughts and impressions were.
Just looking at the name, my feeling was: here we go again, just another flash-in-the-pan internet novelty.

As an old-timer who lived through the dial-up era, I’ve witnessed countless technological advances and innovations. Yet most of these beautiful-looking products simply vanished without a sound into the ancient graves of data.
Just another new chatbot—surely nothing to write home about.

But why was everyone still talking about ChatGPT a week later?

That was strange, wasn't it?

So, I decided to give it a try.

A plain chat interface, nothing magical about it. I threw a few random questions at it, and it did answer them with fluent ease. Okay, interesting enough.
But at that point, I couldn't really think of any practical use for it.
Everything it could provide could be found on Google, and with better real-time accuracy.

That was my first impression of using ChatGPT. A tool that wasn't of much use yet.

Then, before long, things around me started to change.

## How Does Everyone Know About It?

Suddenly, it seemed like everyone knew about ChatGPT, everyone was asking about ChatGPT, and everyone wanted to try ChatGPT.

Friends and relatives from far away were asking me for accounts to use ChatGPT. I had to register multiple new Google accounts just so they could experience it for themselves.

Nothing like this had ever happened before. I had never seen a new internet tool spread at such a breakneck speed.

During this time, I also realized that ChatGPT could actually solve a problem that had been nagging me for ages.
It could help me write in English.

It was an absolute godsend. Even though it couldn't write high-quality prose yet, it was a massive help for me.
Usually, when writing a document or an email, I had to scratch my head and fret over every single word. Now, with just a few simple prompts, it could generate the entire content.

As versions kept updating, more AI Chatbots appeared on the market: Gemini, Claude, Llama, and so on.
Functionally, they were mostly the same, and none of them had yet changed my fundamental perspective.

Until a new thing came along and changed my mind.
